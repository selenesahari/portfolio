Thresholds of Her

Thresholds of Her is a visual sequence tracing how a woman reclaims authorship — not through spectacle, but through presence.

In response to Crossings – Memory, Territory, Movement, this series maps thresholds — physical, emotional, and epistemological: across gardens, deserts, snowfields, city walls, and machine corridors. It traces a sovereign arc — not to be seen, but to arrive.

Spanning Morocco, France, Canada, and the United States, the work documents neither tourism nor retreat — but a movement of return. Through stance, silence, and gaze, the woman resists performance and renders the external gaze obsolete.

Each frame is a threshold — not just crossed, but re-authored. What began as a visual inquiry now reads as cartography: a map of how sovereignty moves through space, silence, refusal — and remains intact.

This project was submitted to Rencontres de la Photographie Marrakech 5th Edition + Festival OFF Arles 2025.
