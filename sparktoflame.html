Spark to Flame

Mantras to Anchor the Feelings

  • “This feeling is me, not them.” – Whenever a spark of attraction or admiration happens, recognize it’s your own vitality waking up, not something they own.
  • “Nothing shakes me—I stand in my own axis.” – A fast, one-line reset that cuts through fog, anxiety, or longing.
  • “I savor the spark, but I am the flame.” – Keeps the good high-energy state but reminds you the source is within.
  • “People come, people go—their absence is freedom, not loss.” – Detaches you from others’ presence or absence.
  • “I am the pillar; the wind passes, but I do not move.” – A visualization mantra (paired with the marble courtyard image).

How to Capture the Feelings

  • Pause when the spark happens: Instead of running with fantasy, freeze the moment. Feel the warmth in your body, name it, and tell yourself: “This is me, I’m keeping this.”
  • Anchor with breath or touch: Place your hand on your chest or make a small circle with your fingers. Link the feeling to a slow, deep breath.
  • Replay and store: Later, recall the memory like a movie: see, feel, pause at the peak. Breathe in and imagine storing it as a golden flame in your chest.
  • Recreate on demand: Play a song, stand tall, recall the image of yourself being “seen.” With practice, the brain no longer needs the external trigger.
  • Extract the essence: Ask: “What quality in them awakened me?” Then practice growing that quality in yourself.

The Key Shift

The feeling was never about them. They were a spark. You are the flame. Once you see this, you can recreate that “in love with life” state at will, without being hooked.
