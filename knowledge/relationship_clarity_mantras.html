When You Don't Know If They Are Choosing You or Using You

The Deep Danger: What if you think someone is rowing with you, but in reality, they’re only using your ship to reach their own shore?

1. The Core Problem

Love is blind when it wants to believe. Our longing makes us project:

“They choose me” when really, “They choose what I can give.”

Using looks like rowing at first. They mimic effort just enough to get the ship moving in their direction.

2. The Test of Intent

How do you know if someone is truly choosing you? Look for alignment in three things:

  • Consistency of Action: Do they still row when it’s inconvenient, slow, or stormy?
  • Reciprocity: Is there a balance between giving and taking over time? Or is it always tilted toward their gain?
  • Transparency: Can you talk about direction openly? Or do they avoid clarity so you can’t hold them to anything?

3. Why This Matters

When someone uses you, you end up rowing their dream while yours sinks. You are the ship. They are just a passenger.

The Harsh Truth: If someone wants you only for what you give, their “love” ends the moment the giving stops.

This is why sovereignty is crucial: When you’re whole, you can see patterns faster. You can stop rowing the moment you feel the imbalance, before you sink.

A Quiet Practice:

When in doubt, ask:

  • If I stopped giving, would they still choose me?
  • If there was no benefit, would they still be here?

Most answers appear silently, without needing confrontation.

When You’re Tied and Willing to Give Just to Hang On

1. What’s Really Happening

Fear overrides clarity. You know on some level the bond is unbalanced, but you’re terrified of losing them.

You start to trade yourself for proximity. Time, money, energy, self-respect—all spent just to keep them from leaving.

2. Why This Happens

  • Attachment wounds: “If I’m enough, they won’t leave.”
  • Scarcity mindset: Belief that this person is the only chance for love or connection.
  • Identity fusion: You start to believe you have no self without them.

3. The Cost

The more you give to preserve them, the less you have of yourself. They don’t respect it—because deep down, they know it’s driven by fear, not strength. Over time, the relationship becomes hostage-taking, not partnership.

4. The Hard Truth

Love given to avoid abandonment is no longer love. It is bargaining. And bargaining rarely makes someone stay out of real choice.

5. The Way Out

Rebuild your own anchor: Even a little bit of self-trust starts breaking the chain.

Ask the hard question: If I stopped giving, what would be left between us?

Shift from fear-based giving to choice-based giving: Give because you want to, not because you’re terrified to lose.

A Metaphor: If you hold onto someone in the sea because you fear drowning, you may both sink. Sometimes letting go is the only way for both to find a shore.

Key Insight: Clinging feels like love but is actually fear wearing a mask. True love cannot grow in a grip. It grows in open hands.

Mantras to Cut Through the Fog

1. Long Clarity Mantra (Slow, Grounding)

Use when you feel attachment panic:

“If I need you, it is not love.
If I cling, I sink.
I choose to see.
I choose myself first.
Only from fullness do I offer my hand.”

How to Use It

  • Pause – When you feel panic at the thought of losing someone.
  • Breathe – Say it slowly, one line per breath.
  • See – Ask: Am I choosing them from strength, or clinging from fear?
  • Act from clarity – If it’s fear, step back. If it’s strength, proceed freely.

Why It Works: It separates need from love, reminds you clinging sinks both boats, and resets focus: you are the source, not them.

2. Short Sword-Strike Mantra (Fast, Sharp)

Use like a mental blade to cut through fog:

“Clinging sinks.
Choice sets free.”

How to Use

  • Morning: Read the long mantra once slowly.
  • In moments of fear: Whisper the short one.
  • Evening: Write one sentence: Where did I act from choice today?

3. Core Relationship Mantra

“I do not seek rescue.
I do not grip.
I see what is, not what I wish.
Love is a choice, not a chain.
I remain whole, with or without you.”

This mantra strips away fantasy, reminds you of sovereignty, and clarifies the distinction between love (choice) and attachment (fear).

Short Version

“See clearly. Choose freely. Stay whole.”

4. Three Mantras to Cut Through Relationship Fog

A. Clarity (Seeing the Truth)

“I see what is,
not what I wish.
I stand with eyes open.”

Use when you feel confusion about someone’s intentions or sense projection.

B. Detachment (Breaking the Grip)

“I release the chain.
Love is a choice, not a grip.
I am whole even alone.”

Use when you feel desperate to hold onto someone, scared they’ll leave.

C. Power (Returning to Self)

“I row my own ship.
If we row together, it is by choice.
If not, I sail on.”

Use when you need to reclaim energy and stop over-giving.

5. Daily 5-Minute Practice to Deprogram Illusions

  • Step 1 – Sit (1 min)
    Close your eyes. Breathe slowly. Imagine yourself standing alone on a wide shore. You are safe, solid.
  • Step 2 – Recite (3 min)
    Choose one mantra above. Speak it slowly, three times. Let the words sink into your chest. Visualize the fog around you clearing.
  • Step 3 – Ask (1 min)
    “Am I rowing because I choose, or because I fear losing them?”

Practice Outcome: Less reactivity, clearer patterns in relationships, and moving from need to choice.
