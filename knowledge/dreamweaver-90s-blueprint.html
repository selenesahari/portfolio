6. Light Bones, Light Mind

Releasing emotional baggage isn’t just spiritual — it’s physical. Emotional stress affects your hormones, posture, digestion, and bone health. To travel light in your 90s, you must lighten your emotional load today.

Why It Matters

  • Stress weakens bones: chronically high cortisol pulls calcium from the bones and slows repair.
  • Emotional lightness improves posture: grief and resentment round the spine; clarity and peace open it.
  • Calm nerves = better digestion: your body absorbs more nutrients when the mind is still.

Daily Practices to Stay Light

  • Mental hygiene: journal worries, then close the book. Don’t carry them all day.
  • Breathwork: try 4–7–8 breathing (inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8) to reduce cortisol.
  • Movement as release: walking, yoga, or tai chi while letting go of stuck thoughts.
  • Boundaries: choose peace over people-pleasing to keep your nervous system clean.
  • Reframing: Ask yourself: “Does this belong to me?” If not, let it go.
“I travel light, outside and inside. My bones carry only me — not my past.”

7. Bone-Strong Routine

Daily practices to keep your bones strong and agile for travel well into your 90s:

1. Daily Nutrition for Strong Bones

  • Calcium-rich foods: dark leafy greens (kale, bok choy, collards), salmon, almonds, tahini, sesame, fortified plant-based milks
  • Vitamin D: 15–20 minutes of sunlight daily or supplementation if deficient
  • Magnesium & Vitamin K2: nuts, seeds, whole grains, avocados, fermented foods (natto, aged cheese)
  • Protein: adequate intake to maintain bone structure

2. Movement (Bones Respond to Load)

  • Weight-bearing exercises: walking, hiking, dancing
  • Strength training: 2–3x per week using bodyweight, bands, or light weights
  • Balance & flexibility: yoga, tai chi, Pilates

Motion sends a signal to the bones to stay strong. Sedentary life = silent bones.

3. Lifestyle Factors

  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol.
  • Avoid overly restrictive diets.
  • Keep posture tall: balanced load keeps bones aligned.

4. Hormonal Health

  • After midlife, monitor bone health with DEXA scans.
  • Track Vitamin D, calcium, and protein levels with your physician.
