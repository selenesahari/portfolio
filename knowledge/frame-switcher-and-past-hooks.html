Frame Switcher – Quick Reference

HOOK:

"Why don’t you post/share? We want to enjoy too."

Hidden Frame:
“If you don’t share, you’re denying others happiness.”

Frame Shift:
“I enjoy things more when I keep them just for myself.”

HOOK:

"You’re selfish for being private."

Hidden Frame:
“Privacy equals selfishness.”

Frame Shift:
“Privacy and selfishness are two different things.”

HOOK:

"How can you work well with others if you’re so private?"

Hidden Frame:
“Personal openness is required for teamwork.”

Frame Shift:
“Privacy is personal. Collaboration is professional.”

HOOK:

"You must not trust us if you don’t share."

Hidden Frame:
“Privacy equals distrust.”

Frame Shift:
“Privacy is a personal choice, not a reflection of trust.”

HOOK:

"Why so secretive? Are you hiding something?"

Hidden Frame:
“If you’re private, there must be something wrong.”

Frame Shift:
“I just prefer to live quietly. It works better for me.”

HOOK:

"If you don’t talk about it, people will assume things."

Hidden Frame:
“You need to manage others’ opinions by explaining yourself.”

Frame Shift:
“People can assume what they want. I’m fine with that.”

HOOK:

"You should share more so people can learn from you!"

Hidden Frame:
“Sharing is an obligation.”

Frame Shift:
“I share selectively. Some things are better kept as lived experiences.”

Protocol

  • Spot the assumption.
  • Step out of their box.
  • Deliver your one-sentence frame shift.
  • Stop. (Do not explain. Let silence hold the new frame.)
“I don’t fight inside their map.
I redraw the map and stand there.”

Past Hook Example: “You Owe Me Rent”

When an ex-boyfriend (or anyone from the past) says something like
“You owe me rent” (about a time when you lived together), it’s another hook.

It’s meant to:

  • Pull you back into an old dynamic
  • Create guilt
  • Test if they still have power over you

Key Principles

  • Do not get pulled into rehashing the past.
  • Do not debate fairness or details.
  • Give a clear, final response — once.

3 Approaches (Choose One)

1. Clean and Final (Best Default)
“I don’t agree with that. That chapter is closed.”
(Short. Calm. No further engagement.)

2. Practical Closure (if they press for money)
“I don’t owe you rent. Please do not contact me about this again.”
If they continue, block or filter.
No emotional language — just a boundary.

3. Velvet Curtain (if you want to be extra soft)
“We both know that time is over. I’m not revisiting it.”
(Said once, then change topic or end the interaction.)

Why This Works

You refuse the frame:
(The frame is: you owe, you’re guilty, defend yourself.)

You close the loop without energy leakage.

Mental Frame

“This is an expired story.
I am not available for re-entry into old contracts.”

If you respond once and cleanly, you don’t need to explain further.
Continuing the conversation = feeding the hook.
